# KK Carousel Formats — 100 Templates

> Library of 100 LinkedIn **carousel / native-document** post-format templates. Companion to
> `kk-post.md` (single text posts). The [bracketed stage directions] are the FORMAT — never change
> them. During `/onboard` (the Taste Interview) a worked EXAMPLE in your voice gets written beneath
> each direction, exactly the way `kk-post.md` is filled. Until then the directions stand alone.
>
> **Why this file exists.** Native document/carousel posts are currently the top-performing LinkedIn
> format for both reach and engagement (deep-research, Jun 2026: AuthoredUp ~3M personal-profile
> posts → documents ≈ 1.39x reach / 1.30x engagement vs a profile's own median; Socialinsider 2026 →
> native document 7.0% engagement, the #1 format). A carousel is NOT a text post chopped into slides.
> Slide 1 carries the hook, each slide holds one idea, and the deck builds to a payoff worth SAVING
> (a save is worth ~5x a like as a reach signal).
>
> **When filling the brackets, apply the "HUMAN TEXTURE" section of `.claude/rules/anti-ai.md`:** open
> on a moment of recognition, keep it concrete, write to one real person, stay inside the bans (no em
> dashes, no AI vocabulary, close on a firm point not a trailing "…"). The format gives structure;
> the texture makes it sound like the owner, not an AI.

## How to read these
Each template has a **[Caption]** direction (the text intro that ships with the carousel — its first
two lines gate the scroll) and a numbered **slide sequence**. The directions ARE the template. Fill a
worked example beneath each during onboarding. Index is grouped into 12 families so no two overlap.

## ★ Save-optimized "reference asset" templates (the 5x-reach play)
A SAVE is worth ~5x a like as a reach signal, and the deck most likely to be saved is a **self-contained
reference asset** — something the reader bookmarks to come back to, not just reads once. The flagship is
**C101 (The Reference-Asset Carousel)**. The rest of the save-bait cluster: **C7** (cheat-sheet stat
stack), **C15** (template/swipe-file), **C18** (audit), **C41–C50** (the whole frameworks/models family),
**C48** (cheat-sheet), **C82** (FAQ), **C95** (proverbs). When the goal is reach via saves, reach for
these first; when the goal is a DM/lead, reach for Family J. Both can be true in one deck.

## Carousel principles (govern every template)
1. **Slide 1 IS the hook.** One bold claim or one sharp tension. No title-card fluff. Earn the swipe.
2. **One idea per slide.** A slide the eye finishes in ~2 seconds. White space is a feature.
3. **8–12 slides is the sweet spot** (5–15 fine). Don't pad to a number.
4. **Design for the SAVE.** The deck should be worth keeping: a checklist, a framework, a sequence.
5. **The caption's first two lines** stop the scroll before slide 1 is even seen. Hook them too.
6. **Close on the CTA slide, not a question.** Earn a save, a keyword, a follow, or a DM. CTAs occasional, not every deck.
7. **Concrete, real numbers, lived examples.** If a slide could come from ChatGPT, it fails. Never invent — leave `[xxx — needs your real X]`.
8. **No overlap, ever.** Each carousel stands on its own story and angle. Themes recur; illustrations don't.
9. **Compliance.** Examples are CAREER/COACHING (Mode 1). Financial topics (numbers, returns, products, rankings) flip to Mode 2 — bucket B/C → pre-approval or NOT allowed per `references/iafa-compliance.md`. Default: carousels for coaching/career; financial stays conservative text. When in doubt → HOLD.
10. **Run qa-gate before shipping.** Voice, anti-AI, compliance, cadence.

> reads as high value and pushes wider. The rule that separates this from an ordinary listicle or how-to:
> the deck must be USABLE on its own, without you, again and again. It's a tool, not a read.
