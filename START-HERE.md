# How To Use This Workspace (Plain-English Manual)

Welcome. This folder is your own LinkedIn helper. It lives on your computer. Nothing
runs in the cloud and nothing gets sent without you. Think of it as a smart assistant
that writes drafts for you. You read them, you decide, and you press send yourself.

This page explains the whole thing in simple words. Read it once and you will know what
to do.

## What this workspace is for

You want to find the right people on LinkedIn and turn them into real conversations.
Some of those talks lead to a booked call. Some lead to a partnership. This workspace
helps you do that, one small step at a time.

Here is the path you are walking. Read it left to right:

```
comment on their posts  →  connect  →  chat in DMs  →  find their problem
  →  offer a free helper (lead magnet)  →  send 3 short messages  →  book a call  →  close
```

You do not have to remember all of that. The workspace remembers it for you.

## The one big rule

The assistant only writes **drafts**. It never sends anything for you. You copy the
words, you paste them into LinkedIn, and you hit send. You are always in control.

A few more rules that keep your account safe and your voice real:

- Everything you do gets **written down** for you (in the trackers and the audit log).
- **Never sell in a DM.** A DM has one job: earn the call. Selling comes later.
- The writing sounds like **you**, not like a robot. No fancy filler words, no fake
  flattery, no dashes in the middle of sentences.

## How to "talk" to the workspace

You give it commands. A command starts with a slash, like `/start-day`. You type the
command and the assistant does that job. You do not need to learn code. Each command is
just a short word for a task.

## First time here? Do this once

Run **`/onboard`**.

This is the setup wizard. It asks you simple questions about yourself: who you are, who
you want to reach, what you offer, and how you like to sound. It fills in the workspace
with your answers so every draft later sounds like you.

You can stop in the middle and come back. Just run `/onboard` again and it picks up where
you left off. There is a short version (about 20 minutes) and a longer, deeper version you
can do any time.

## Your daily routine

Once you are set up, most days look like this. Five steps, in order.

1. **`/start-day`** — The assistant reads your trackers and writes today's to-do list.
   It tells you who to reach out to and who you owe a reply.

2. **Work the list.** Pick the command that fits the task in front of you:
   - **`/commenting-others`** — Helps you leave good comments on other people's posts.
   - **`/dm-sales-coach`** — Tells you what to say to a lead in your messages.
   - **`/respond-to-own`** — Helps you reply to comments on your own posts.

3. **`/log-prospect`** — Whenever something changes (you sent a request, they replied,
   a call got booked), tell this command and it writes it down.

4. **Want to post something?** Run **`/post`** to write a LinkedIn post. Need a free
   giveaway to attract leads? Run **`/lead-magnet-gen`**.

5. **Hit a weird case the playbook does not cover?** Run **`/amend-sop`**. It saves a
   note so the playbook can get smarter later.

6. **Heard or remembered something good?** Run **`/capture`**. Use it to save your own
   stories, a strong opinion you hold, or a nice thing someone said about you. The
   workspace files it in the right place so your future posts and messages can use it.

That is the whole loop. Do it most days and your pipeline fills up.

## Where things live (only if you are curious)

You can ignore this part at first. But if you want to peek under the hood:

- **`account-profile.md`** — Your identity and your offer.
- **`kk-post.md`** — Your post styles.
- **`trackers/`** — The spreadsheets that track your leads and your engagement.
- **`lead-magnets/`** — Your free giveaways.
- **`references/`** — Deeper notes about your voice, your story, and your targets.
- **`sops/linkedin-sop.md`** — The full playbook, if you ever want the long version.

## If you only remember three things

1. Run **`/onboard`** first. Run **`/start-day`** every day after that.
2. The assistant writes drafts. **You** send them.
3. When in doubt, just type the slash command for the task you want and let it guide you.

That is it. You are ready. Start with `/onboard`.
